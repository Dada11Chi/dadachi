Celtic Tree Astrology

Every tree reflects certain qualities, phases of life and soul lessons – like guardians at the threshold between the visible and the invisible.

Here you can discover which tree energy you were born under – and what it might reveal about your personality, your challenges and your gifts.

  • The Celts loved the forest and the trees. They knew that everything on Earth is interconnected – that everything exchanges information with everything in order to grow and prosper. Hence, they arranged the year in a tree circle and placed each tree in the time of the year that best suited its natural characteristics.

    The Celtic Tree Astrology available today is not a direct tradition, but was re-created in the 20th century, based on the remnants of Celtic knowledge. Because of this strange way of origin, there are two different circles in use: an inner and an outer tree circle.

    If you find a different horoscope here than you know, that’s because only the inner circle is illuminated here, as in it – in contrast to the outer circle – no tree is repeated.

    The best way to tune in to the wisdom of these signs is to meditate on their strengths. Like this, you train your astral body and become capable at some point – instead of reading secular books – to read directly in Akash, the library of the Otherworld, which contains every information about everything.

DEC 24 - JAN 20

BIRCH

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Birch is aligned with the Deer — a symbol of grace, quiet strength, and new beginnings. The archetype is The High-Flyer, one who rises with vision and purpose, often leading the way into uncharted territory.

It corresponds to the following themes: Growth, Stability, Renewal, Initiation, Adaptability, and Transformation.

Birch carries the energy of fresh starts and clear direction. Those guided by this tree often bring light into dark places — not by force, but by simply growing upward, steadily, toward what calls them.

Their strength lies in their adaptability. Birch souls bend without breaking, grounded in stability yet open to change. They often experience life as a series of initiations — moments of renewal that push them to shed old skin and step into greater truth.

This is the path of the High-Flyer — graceful, resilient, and quietly determined. Guided by the gentleness of the Deer and the regenerative power of the Birch, they are here to lead with clarity, and to remind others that true growth always begins within.

JAN 21 - FEB 17

ROWAN

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Rowan corresponds to the Dragon — a creature of ancient power, mysticism, and hidden knowledge. The archetype here is The Thinker: one who sees beyond appearances and walks between worlds with vision and clarity.

It corresponds to the following themes: Power, Mysticism, Vision, Connection, Change, Balance, Healing, and Protection.

Rowan carries the energy of transformation and protection. Those touched by this tree are often agents of change — not through force, but through insight. Their minds are sharp, their hearts attuned to balance, and their presence can be quietly healing.

There is a natural connection to the unseen, a mystic thread that runs through their thoughts and dreams. When in alignment, Rowan energy bridges intellect and intuition, helping others see clearly — and remember their own inner fire.

This is the path of the visionary thinker, guided by the wisdom of the Dragon and the resilience of the Rowan tree.

FEB 18 - MAR 17

ASH

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Ash is aligned with the Otter — a symbol of playfulness, fluidity, and intuitive wisdom. The archetype is The Charmer, one who moves through life with grace, curiosity, and a natural ability to inspire others.

It corresponds to the following themes: Roots, Growth, Expansion, Resurrection and Higher Perspective.

Ash carries the energy of deep connection and upward movement. Those touched by this tree often have strong inner roots — anchoring them in unseen realms — while simultaneously reaching outward into new possibilities. They are dreamers and visionaries, able to sense what wants to emerge before it takes form.

There is a natural charm in Ash energy, not from performance, but from authenticity. These souls grow through experience, expanding their awareness as they explore life’s mysteries. Even after setbacks, they know how to begin again — resurrection is in their bones.

This is the path of the Charmer: playful, profound, and deeply connected to both earth and sky. Guided by the magic of the Otter and the expansive spirit of the Ash, they remind us that growth can be joyful — and that transformation begins when we trust the current.

MAR 18 - APR 14

ALDER

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Alder is aligned with the Fox — a symbol of cleverness, agility, and quiet courage. The archetype is The Pioneer, one who moves into the unknown with instinct, insight, and a fierce connection to purpose.

It corresponds to the following themes: Inner Fire, Higher Self, Conversion, Transmutation, Hidden Powers, and Spirit of Discovery.

Alder carries the energy of transformation through action. Those born under this tree often blaze trails — not by following maps, but by trusting their inner fire to light the way. They are attuned to the voice of the Higher Self and often called to walk paths others can’t yet see.

This energy is bold but inwardly rooted. Alder souls hold hidden powers that awaken when they’re pushed beyond the familiar. Through challenge, they convert fear into courage, and resistance into fuel for growth.

This is the path of the Pioneer — alert, intuitive, and unafraid to change form. Guided by the sharp instincts of the Fox and the transmuting force of Alder, they are natural explorers of both inner and outer worlds, reminding us that discovery begins the moment we choose to leap.

APR 14 - MAY 12

WILLOW

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Willow is aligned with the Hare — a symbol of lunar wisdom, sensitivity, and hidden strength. The archetype is The Observer, one who perceives the subtle layers of reality and moves through life with quiet depth.

It corresponds to the following themes: Medial Skills, Moon, Intuition, Strength, Love, Imagination, Symbiosis, Growth, Trance, and Belief.

Willow carries the energy of deep listening and inner vision. Those born under this sign often have a natural connection to the moon and its rhythms — feeling the pull of tides both emotional and spiritual. Their intuitive gifts are strong, often expressing through dreams, creativity, or silent knowing.

Though gentle in nature, Willow souls hold a quiet strength. Their love runs deep, their imagination vast, and their presence brings harmony where dissonance once ruled. In relationship with others and the unseen world, they thrive in symbiosis — exchanging energy, wisdom, and care.

This is the path of the Observer — tender, perceptive, and profoundly in tune. Guided by the nimble spirit of the Hare and the graceful flexibility of the Willow, they remind us that truth is often found not in action, but in awareness.

MAY 13 - JUN 09

HAWTHORN

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Hawthorn is aligned with the Owl — a creature of twilight and mystery, able to see what others overlook. The archetype is The Illusionist, one who dances between realities, revealing truth through paradox and play.

It corresponds to the following themes: Duality, Balance, Diversity, Medicine, Inconsistency, and Union of Opposites.

Hawthorn carries the energy of sacred contradiction. Those born under this sign often embody seeming opposites — light and shadow, joy and sorrow, depth and lightness — all coexisting in one being. They are shapeshifters in spirit, able to navigate complexity with grace.

This sign teaches that truth is rarely simple, and that healing often comes from embracing what we were taught to resist. Hawthorn souls hold medicine in their ability to weave together contradictions, to find balance in movement, and to reflect the world’s hidden layers.

This is the path of the Illusionist — mystical, wise, and ever-changing. Guided by the silent flight of the Owl and the thorned beauty of the Hawthorn, they remind us that nothing is only what it seems… and that wholeness lives in the union of opposites.

JUN 10 - JUL 07

OAK

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Oak is aligned with the Wren — a small yet powerful bird, symbolizing wisdom, tradition, and sacred song. The archetype is The Stabilizer, one who anchors, protects, and carries the old knowledge forward.

It corresponds to the following themes: Honor, Family, Loyalty, Strength, Wisdom, Longevity, and Inheritance.

Oak carries the energy of grounded power and steady presence. Those born under this sign often serve as pillars in their communities or families — deeply rooted, trustworthy, and quietly influential. They honor tradition, yet hold the wisdom to know when to adapt and evolve.

With strong ties to ancestry and legacy, Oak souls are carriers of inherited strength — not just of bloodline, but of soul lineage. Their loyalty runs deep, and their sense of responsibility is matched by a heart that values fairness and truth.

This is the path of the Stabilizer — wise, dependable, and enduring. Guided by the timeless voice of the Wren and the noble stature of the Oak, they remind us that real strength is not loud or showy, but quietly unwavering.

JUL 08 - AUG 04

HOLLY

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Holly is aligned with the Unicorn — a mythical creature of purity, power, and untamed grace. The archetype is The Ruler, one who leads not by dominance, but by presence, purpose, and inner sovereignty.

It corresponds to the following themes: Strength, Challenge, Joy of Life, Protection, Life Energy and Inner Beauty.

Holly carries the energy of noble leadership and vibrant vitality. Those born under this sign are often naturally strong — not just in will, but in heart. They meet life’s challenges head-on, turning obstacles into opportunities for growth and transformation.

There is a deep well of life energy within Holly souls, paired with a powerful sense of protection — for others, for truth, and for what is sacred. Their presence uplifts, inspires, and defends. At their core, they embody the joyful force of life itself.

This is the path of the Ruler — radiant, resilient, and rooted in integrity. Guided by the mythical light of the Unicorn and the steadfast spirit of the Holly tree, they remind us that true power begins with self-mastery and the courage to lead with love.

AUG 05 - SEP 01

HAZEL

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Hazel is aligned with the Salmon — the ancient keeper of wisdom, intuition, and sacred flow. The archetype is The Knower, one who drinks from the deep well of insight and brings back clarity, creativity, and truth.

It corresponds to the following themes: Visions, Instinct, Intellect, Creativity, Inspiration, Knowledge, and Mindfulness.

Hazel carries the energy of insight made tangible. Those born under this sign often have a natural gift for learning, synthesizing, and expressing ideas — whether through words, art, or subtle perception. Their minds are bright, but their wisdom flows from the heart.

They move between intuition and intellect with ease, guided by flashes of inspiration and a quiet trust in their inner compass. Hazel souls are natural teachers, storytellers, and idea-bearers, offering fresh perspectives rooted in timeless knowing.

This is the path of the Knower — perceptive, imaginative, and deeply thoughtful. Guided by the ancient wisdom of the Salmon and the luminous clarity of the Hazel tree, they remind us that true knowledge is not just gathered — it is embodied and shared.

SEP 02 - SEP 29

VINE

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Vine is aligned with the Swan — a symbol of grace, transition, and the soul’s quiet majesty. The archetype is The Equalizer, one who brings harmony where there is imbalance, and movement where there is stagnation.

It corresponds to the following themes: Fertility, Chance, Donation, Connection, Continuation, Enlargement, and Regeneration.

Vine carries the energy of unfolding potential and sacred flow. Those born under this sign often thrive in cycles — of growth, letting go, and rebirth. They have a gift for finding beauty in in-between spaces, where endings become beginnings.

Their presence brings a sense of natural connection — to others, to the Earth, to the deeper rhythm of life. Vine souls are generous by nature, offering time, energy, or insight without expectation. They understand that what is given returns, often in unexpected ways.

This is the path of the Equalizer — flowing, fertile, and quietly powerful. Guided by the elegance of the Swan and the ever-growing nature of the Vine, they remind us that life is not linear, but spiraling — and every turn holds the promise of renewal.

SEP 30 - OCT 27

IVY

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Ivy is aligned with the Butterfly — a symbol of transformation, lightness, and the resilience of the soul. The archetype is The Survivor, one who endures with grace and rises stronger after every fall.

It corresponds to the following themes: Growth, Change, Renewal, Friendship, and Connection.

Ivy carries the energy of quiet perseverance and sacred transformation. Those born under this sign know how to move through life’s twists and turns with flexibility and faith. Like the Ivy plant itself, they find a way — climbing, weaving, adapting — without losing their essence.

These individuals are deeply relational, valuing true friendship and emotional connection. They remind us that strength can be soft, and that lasting growth often comes through trust, intimacy, and shared journeying.

This is the path of the Survivor — gentle, enduring, and ever-evolving. Guided by the delicate power of the Butterfly and the tenacious spirit of Ivy, they teach us that transformation is not just possible — it is inevitable, and beautiful.

OCT 28 - NOV 24

REED

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Reed is aligned with the Dog — a symbol of loyalty, guidance, and deep emotional intelligence. The archetype is The Inquisitor, one who seeks truth beneath the surface and brings clarity through careful listening and questioning.

It corresponds to the following themes: Purpose, Intention, Protection, Purification, Clarification, and Communication.

Reed carries the energy of focused truth-seeking and inner refinement. Those born under this sign often have a natural instinct to go beyond appearances — to ask the deeper questions, to sense what’s unspoken, and to bring light to what lies hidden.

Their strength lies in their unwavering intention and loyalty to what is real. They are protectors of sacred space — within themselves and in their connections with others — and they often serve as clear channels for meaningful communication and insight.

This is the path of the Inquisitor — intuitive, precise, and purpose-driven. Guided by the devoted heart of the Dog and the purifying presence of the Reed, they remind us that clarity is a form of healing, and truth, when spoken with care, can be a sacred act.

NOV 25 - DEC 23

ELDER

In the Celtic Tree Astrology, Elder is aligned with the Raven — a symbol of mystery, transformation, and the deep magic of endings and beginnings. The archetype is The Seeker, one who walks willingly into the unknown in search of truth, depth, and renewal.

It corresponds to the following themes: Death, Cycles, Change, Rebirth, Renewal, Creativity, and Regeneration.

Elder carries the energy of powerful transitions and soul evolution. Those born under this sign often move through life in distinct chapters, each one marked by deep inner change. They are not afraid of the dark — they know it is where the next light is born.

With a natural connection to life’s hidden rhythms, Elder souls are often drawn to spiritual truths, creative expression, and the mysteries of transformation. They carry within them the wisdom of what has been — and the spark of what is yet to come.

This is the path of the Seeker — brave, creative, and endlessly renewing. Guided by the watchful eye of the Raven and the ever-turning wheel of the Elder tree, they remind us that every ending holds the seed of new life… and every seeker finds, in time, what they were born to remember.
